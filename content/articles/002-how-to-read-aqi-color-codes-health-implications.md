# How to Read AQI: Color Codes & Health Implications

**Meta Description:** Learn how to read AQI color codes, understand health implications at different air quality levels, and make informed decisions for your family's safety.

---

## Introduction

Checking the Air Quality Index (AQI) has become part of daily life for millions of Indians, but simply knowing the number isn't enough. Understanding what the AQI color codes mean and their specific health implications empowers you to make better decisions about outdoor activities, protect vulnerable family members, and take appropriate action when air quality deteriorates.

The color-coded AQI system provides instant visual recognition of air quality levels, but each color carries different health risks and recommended actions. Whether you see green, yellow, orange, red, purple, or maroon on your weather app, understanding these colors can mean the difference between a healthy day and respiratory distress.

This comprehensive guide breaks down each AQI color category, explains the health implications in detail, and provides practical guidance for interpreting AQI readings in your daily life. We'll also explore how different populations—children, elderly, pregnant women, and those with pre-existing conditions—should respond to varying AQI levels.

## The AQI Color Code System

### Understanding the Visual Language

The AQI color system was designed to communicate air quality information quickly and effectively to people of all education levels. When you see a color, you immediately know whether it's safe to go outside, exercise, or take protective measures.

**The six AQI color categories are:**

1. **Green** - Good (0-50)
2. **Yellow** - Satisfactory (51-100)
3. **Orange** - Moderately Polluted (101-200)
4. **Red** - Poor (201-300)
5. **Purple** - Very Poor (301-400)
6. **Maroon** - Severe (401-500+)

Each color corresponds to a specific AQI range and carries distinct health implications. The system is standardized across India through the Central Pollution Control Board (CPCB), ensuring consistency whether you're in Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore, or any other monitored city.

### How Colors Are Determined

The AQI calculation process determines which pollutant is the "dominant" pollutant at any given time. The highest sub-index value among all measured pollutants becomes the overall AQI, and this determines the color code displayed. This means that even if most pollutants are at safe levels, a single high pollutant can trigger a red or purple AQI reading.

## Green: Good Air Quality (AQI 0-50)

### What Green Means

**Air Quality:** Satisfactory, with little to no risk of air pollution effects.

**Health Impact:** 
- Air pollution poses minimal risk to the general population
- Outdoor air quality is safe for everyone, including sensitive groups
- No health effects are expected even with prolonged outdoor exposure

**Visual Appearance:** Green is the most reassuring color on the AQI scale, indicating ideal conditions for all outdoor activities.

### Recommended Actions

**For General Population:**
- Enjoy outdoor activities freely without restrictions
- Exercise outdoors as preferred—running, cycling, sports
- Open windows for natural ventilation
- Spend extra time outside for mental and physical health benefits

**For Sensitive Groups:**
- No restrictions needed
- Ideal conditions for outdoor activities even for those with respiratory conditions
- Good opportunity for outdoor therapy or rehabilitation exercises

### When to Expect Green AQI

Green AQI typically occurs during:
- Monsoon season (July-September) in most regions
- Early morning hours in many cities
- Coastal areas with consistent sea breezes
- Regions away from industrial zones and heavy traffic

---

## Yellow: Satisfactory Air Quality (AQI 51-100)

### What Yellow Means

**Air Quality:** Acceptable, with minimal health concerns for most people.

**Health Impact:**
- Little to no health risk for the general population
- Minor breathing discomfort possible for extremely sensitive individuals
- Air quality is still considered safe for outdoor activities

**Visual Appearance:** Yellow indicates moderate caution but generally safe conditions. Most people can continue normal activities without concern.

### Recommended Actions

**For General Population:**
- Normal outdoor activities are fine
- Continue regular exercise routines
- No restrictions on ventilation or outdoor time
- Good conditions for outdoor events and gatherings

**For Sensitive Groups:**
- Monitor for any breathing discomfort
- May want to reduce prolonged or intense outdoor exertion
- Consider indoor exercise if symptoms develop
- Carry rescue inhalers if prescribed for asthma

**Who Are Sensitive Groups?**
- People with asthma or COPD
- Those with heart conditions
- Elderly individuals
- Pregnant women
- Children (especially those under 5)
- People recovering from respiratory infections

### When Yellow AQI Occurs

Yellow AQI is common in:
- Early morning hours in urban areas
- Areas with moderate traffic but good dispersion
- Transition periods between pollution seasons
- Regions with partial pollution control measures

---

## Orange: Moderately Polluted (AQI 101-200)

### What Orange Means

**Air Quality:** Unhealthy for sensitive groups, acceptable for general population.

**Health Impact:**
- Breathing discomfort for people with lung diseases
- Children, elderly, and heart patients may experience symptoms
- Healthy adults generally unaffected, but prolonged exposure may cause issues
- Outdoor exercise becomes problematic for sensitive individuals

**Visual Appearance:** Orange signals caution and requires attention, especially for vulnerable populations. This is the first level where outdoor activities need modification for some people.

### Health Implications in Detail

**Respiratory Effects:**
- Irritation of airways
- Increased coughing and throat irritation
- Shortness of breath during physical activity
- Exacerbation of asthma symptoms

**Cardiovascular Effects:**
- Increased heart rate and blood pressure
- Potential chest tightness in sensitive individuals
- Reduced oxygen delivery during exercise

**Who Is Most Affected?**
- Asthma patients (increased attacks likely)
- COPD patients (worsening symptoms)
- Elderly (reduced lung capacity)
- Children (developing respiratory systems)
- Heart disease patients (increased cardiac stress)

### Recommended Actions

**For General Population:**
- Continue normal activities with awareness
- Outdoor exercise is generally safe but monitor how you feel
- Consider indoor alternatives if you experience any discomfort

**For Sensitive Groups:**
- Limit prolonged outdoor exertion
- Reduce intensity of outdoor activities
- Plan outdoor time for early morning or evening when AQI may be lower
- Use air purifiers indoors
- Keep rescue medications readily available

**Specific Guidelines:**
- **Asthma patients:** Reduce outdoor exercise; ensure inhalers are accessible
- **Elderly:** Limit outdoor time during peak pollution hours (usually morning and evening)
- **Children:** Encourage indoor play; limit outdoor sports practice
- **Pregnant women:** Limit outdoor exposure; use air purifiers at home

### When Orange AQI Occurs

Orange AQI is frequent in:
- Urban areas with heavy traffic
- Industrial zones
- Areas near construction sites
- Winter mornings in northern India
- Summer afternoons in some cities

---

## Red: Poor Air Quality (AQI 201-300)

### What Red Means

**Air Quality:** Unhealthy for everyone, not just sensitive groups.

**Health Impact:**
- Breathing discomfort for most people
- Healthy individuals may experience respiratory symptoms
- Prolonged exposure can cause health effects even in healthy people
- Outdoor activities become problematic for everyone

**Visual Appearance:** Red is a warning color indicating serious air quality issues. This level requires everyone to modify their outdoor activities and take protective measures.

### Health Implications in Detail

**Respiratory Effects:**
- Coughing and throat irritation in most people
- Increased mucus production
- Shortness of breath even with light activity
- Exacerbation of existing respiratory conditions

**Cardiovascular Effects:**
- Increased strain on the heart
- Higher risk of cardiac events in vulnerable individuals
- Reduced blood oxygen levels during activity

**Systemic Effects:**
- Headaches and fatigue
- Eye irritation
- Reduced lung function even in healthy adults

### Recommended Actions

**For General Population:**
- Reduce prolonged outdoor exertion
- Limit outdoor time to essential activities only
- Avoid outdoor exercise
- Use air purifiers indoors
- Keep windows closed

**For Sensitive Groups:**
- Stay indoors as much as possible
- Avoid all outdoor activities
- Use air purifiers with HEPA filters
- Consult doctors about medication adjustments
- Have emergency plans in place

**Specific Guidelines:**
- **Everyone:** Avoid outdoor sports and exercise
- **Children:** Keep indoors; indoor play only
- **Elderly:** Remain indoors with air purifiers running
- **Outdoor workers:** Limit exposure time; use N95 masks
- **Commute:** Use vehicles with closed windows and air recirculation

### When Red AQI Occurs

Red AQI typically occurs during:
- Peak winter pollution in northern India (November-February)
- Heavy traffic congestion periods
- Industrial accidents or unplanned emissions
- Agricultural burning seasons
- Dust storm events

---

## Purple: Very Poor Air Quality (AQI 301-400)

### What Purple Means

**Air Quality:** Very unhealthy with significant health risks for all populations.

**Health Impact:**
- Respiratory illness likely with prolonged exposure
- Health effects even for healthy individuals
- Emergency conditions for vulnerable populations
- Serious risk of health complications

**Visual Appearance:** Purple signals emergency-level air quality. This color indicates that everyone should avoid outdoor exposure as much as possible.

### Health Implications in Detail

**Immediate Health Effects:**
- Severe respiratory distress
- Chest tightness and breathing difficulties
- Increased risk of asthma attacks and COPD exacerbations
- Potential for emergency room visits

**Long-term Health Risks:**
- Increased risk of respiratory infections
- Cardiovascular stress that may trigger heart events
- Reduced lung function that may persist for days
- Aggravation of chronic health conditions

**Vulnerable Populations at Extreme Risk:**
- Asthma patients (high risk of severe attacks)
- COPD patients (possible hospitalization)
- Heart disease patients (increased cardiac events)
- Elderly (higher mortality risk)
- Children (developmental lung damage possible)

### Recommended Actions

**For General Population:**
- Stay indoors as much as possible
- Avoid all outdoor activities
- Use air purifiers with HEPA filters in all living spaces
- Keep windows and doors closed
- If you must go outside, wear N95 masks

**For Sensitive Groups:**
- Stay indoors completely
- Do not venture outside even with masks
- Ensure air purifiers are running continuously
- Monitor symptoms closely
- Have emergency contact information ready
- Consider temporary relocation if possible

**For Essential Outdoor Workers:**
- Limit exposure time to absolute minimum
- Use properly fitted N95 or N99 masks
- Take frequent indoor breaks
- Report any symptoms immediately

**Home Preparation:**
- Seal windows with tape if necessary
- Run air purifiers on highest setting
- Avoid activities that increase indoor air pollution (cooking with open flames, smoking)
- Keep medication readily available

### When Purple AQI Occurs

Purple AQI is severe and typically occurs during:
- Extreme winter pollution episodes in Delhi-NCR and other northern cities
- Major industrial accidents
- Widespread agricultural burning combined with weather inversion
- Prolonged periods without wind or rain
- Crises requiring emergency health advisories

---

## Maroon: Severe Air Quality (AQI 401-500+)

### What Maroon Means

**Air Quality:** Hazardous with emergency health conditions.

**Health Impact:**
- Serious health effects for everyone
- Emergency conditions requiring immediate protective action
- Significant risk of hospitalization or severe health complications
- Outdoor exposure is dangerous even for short periods

**Visual Appearance:** Maroon is the highest level on the AQI scale, indicating air quality that poses immediate danger to public health. This is a medical emergency situation.

### Health Implications in Detail

**Immediate Health Emergency:**
- Severe breathing difficulties even at rest
- High risk of respiratory failure in vulnerable individuals
- Cardiac emergencies likely in heart patients
- Potential for widespread health crisis

**System-Wide Effects:**
- Emergency room visits increase dramatically
- Hospital admissions for respiratory and cardiac conditions spike
- Work absenteeism increases significantly
- School and business closures may be necessary

**Who Is at Maximum Risk:**
- Anyone with respiratory conditions
- Cardiac patients
- Elderly populations
- Young children
- Pregnant women
- Outdoor workers

### Recommended Actions

**For Everyone:**
- **Stay indoors completely** - do not go outside for any reason
- **Seal indoor environment** - close all windows and doors
- **Run air purifiers continuously** on highest setting
- **Avoid indoor pollution sources** - no smoking, minimal cooking
- **Stay hydrated** - drink plenty of water
- **Monitor symptoms** - seek medical attention if breathing difficulties develop

**For Vulnerable Populations:**
- Consider temporary relocation to cleaner areas if possible
- Have emergency medical plan ready
- Keep all medications accessible
- Stay in contact with healthcare providers
- Avoid any physical exertion

**For Essential Workers:**
- If outdoor work is absolutely necessary:
  - Use professional-grade respirators (N99 or P100)
  - Limit exposure to minutes, not hours
  - Take immediate shelter if symptoms develop
  - Report all health issues to supervisors

**Emergency Preparedness:**
- Keep 3-5 days of supplies indoors
- Have emergency contacts readily available
- Know the location of nearest medical facilities
- Keep first aid kit accessible
- Monitor official advisories continuously

### When Maroon AQI Occurs

Maroon AQI is rare but occurs during:
- Extreme pollution crises (like Delhi's November 2019 episode)
- Major industrial disasters
- Widespread fires or chemical releases
- Complete weather stagnation combined with multiple pollution sources
- Natural disasters affecting air quality

## Interpreting AQI in Daily Life

### Morning Routine Adjustments

**Check AQI Before Morning Exercise:**
- Green/Yellow: Safe for outdoor runs or walks
- Orange: Consider indoor exercise or reduce intensity
- Red/Purple/Maroon: Indoor exercise only

**Ventilation Decisions:**
- Green/Yellow: Open windows for fresh air
- Orange: Limit window opening to short periods
- Red+: Keep windows closed; use air purifiers

### Commute Planning

**Transportation Choices:**
- Green/Yellow: Normal commute, can walk/bike if preferred
- Orange: Consider public transport over walking/biking
- Red+: Use vehicles with closed windows and air recirculation
- Purple/Maroon: Minimize commute time; work from home if possible

**Route Selection:**
- Avoid heavy traffic routes when AQI is elevated
- Choose routes with tree cover when walking/cycling
- Use highways with better ventilation over congested streets

### School and Work Decisions

**For Parents:**
- Green/Yellow: Normal school attendance
- Orange: Keep children indoors during recess; limit outdoor sports
- Red+: Consider keeping children home; ensure school has indoor activities
- Purple/Maroon: Keep children home from school

**For Employers:**
- Green/Yellow: Normal operations
- Orange: Allow flexible schedules; permit work from home for sensitive employees
- Red+: Encourage work from home; limit outdoor work
- Purple/Maroon: Close offices or allow mandatory work from home

## AQI Trends and Forecasting

### Understanding AQI Patterns

**Daily Patterns:**
- Morning AQI often highest due to temperature inversion
- Evening AQI may improve with increased wind or traffic patterns
- Weekend AQI may be lower due to reduced traffic

**Weekly Patterns:**
- Weekday AQI typically higher than weekends
- Monday mornings often show elevated levels
- Friday afternoons may show improvement

**Seasonal Patterns:**
- Winter: Highest AQI due to inversion, biomass burning
- Summer: Moderate AQI with dust storms in some areas
- Monsoon: Best AQI due to rain cleansing
- Post-monsoon: Transition period with varying conditions

### Using AQI Forecasts

**What Forecasts Provide:**
- Expected AQI ranges for next 24-48 hours
- Trend analysis (improving or worsening)
- Peak pollution hours identified
- Weather factors affecting air quality

**How to Use Forecasts:**
- Plan outdoor activities during forecasted lower AQI periods
- Schedule essential outdoor work during better air quality windows
- Prepare for high AQI days with indoor supplies
- Adjust medication schedules if you have respiratory conditions

**Limitations of Forecasts:**
- Weather changes can rapidly alter AQI
- Unexpected events (fires, accidents) can cause sudden spikes
- Forecasts are most reliable for 24-48 hours
- Local variations may differ from regional forecasts

## Special Considerations by Population

### Children and AQI

**Why Children Are More Vulnerable:**
- Developing lungs are more susceptible to damage
- Higher breathing rate per body weight
- More time spent outdoors
- Less ability to recognize and report symptoms

**Guidelines for Different AQI Levels:**

**Green/Yellow:**
- Normal outdoor play encouraged
- Outdoor sports and activities fine
- Develop healthy outdoor habits

**Orange:**
- Limit outdoor playtime
- Indoor activities preferred
- Monitor for coughing or breathing issues
- Ensure school has indoor recess options

**Red:**
- Keep children indoors
- Indoor playdates and activities only
- Avoid outdoor sports completely
- Watch for any respiratory symptoms

**Purple/Maroon:**
- Keep children home from school if possible
- Complete indoor restriction
- Monitor health closely
- Have medical plan ready

### Elderly and AQI

**Special Risks:**
- Reduced lung capacity and function
- Higher likelihood of pre-existing conditions
- More medications that may interact with pollution effects
- Reduced ability to recognize symptoms

**Guidelines:**
- **Green/Yellow:** Normal activities with awareness
- **Orange:** Limit outdoor exertion; monitor symptoms
- **Red:** Stay indoors; use air purifiers
- **Purple/Maroon:** Strict indoor confinement; medical supervision

### Pregnant Women and AQI

**Special Considerations:**
- Air pollution can affect fetal development
- Increased susceptibility to respiratory issues
- Need for extra caution for both mother and baby

**Guidelines:**
- **Green/Yellow:** Normal activities with caution
- **Orange:** Limit outdoor exposure; use air purifiers at home
- **Red:** Minimize outdoor time; consult healthcare provider
- **Purple/Maroon:** Stay indoors; discuss with obstetrician

### People with Pre-existing Conditions

**Asthma Patients:**
- Always carry rescue inhaler
- Monitor AQI daily
- Adjust medication schedule as advised by doctor
- Have written action plan for high AQI days

**COPD Patients:**
- Avoid all outdoor activities when AQI >100
- Use oxygen therapy if prescribed
- Have emergency plan ready
- Monitor symptoms closely

**Heart Disease Patients:**
- Avoid outdoor exercise when AQI >100
- Monitor blood pressure regularly
- Have medication readily available
- Seek immediate care for chest pain or severe symptoms

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How do I know which AQI color to trust when different apps show different values?

Different monitoring stations and calculation methods can produce slightly different AQI values. For the most accurate local AQI, check the official CPCB website or state pollution control board. If multiple sources show similar trends (improving or worsening), that trend is more important than the exact number.

### What should I do if I need to go outside during Red AQI?

If outdoor activity is unavoidable during Red AQI:
- Wear a properly fitted N95 mask
- Limit time outside to absolute minimum
- Avoid physical exertion
- Take breaks indoors frequently
- Shower and change clothes immediately upon returning indoors

### Can AQI colors change throughout the day?

Yes, AQI typically changes throughout the day. Morning AQI is often highest due to temperature inversion, while afternoon may improve with increased wind and vertical mixing. Check AQI multiple times daily when air quality is poor.

### How accurate are AQI color codes for my specific location?

AQI readings are most accurate for the specific monitoring station location. Conditions can vary within a city due to local factors like traffic, industry, and topography. Use local readings when possible and consider the general trend rather than exact values.

### Are indoor air purifiers effective during high AQI days?

Yes, quality air purifiers with HEPA filters can significantly reduce indoor PM2.5 levels during high AQI days. However, they must be properly sized for the room and run continuously. Windows and doors should remain closed to prevent polluted outdoor air from entering.

### When should I seek medical attention during high AQI days?

Seek immediate medical attention if you experience:
- Severe breathing difficulties
- Chest pain or tightness
- Bluish lips or fingernails
- Confusion or disorientation
- Severe coughing that doesn't stop
- Symptoms that don't improve with medication
