## What is AQI? The Basics

### Definition and Purpose

The Air Quality Index (AQI) is a numerical scale used to communicate the quality of outdoor air to the general public. It converts measurements of multiple air pollutants into a single, easy-to-understand number that indicates the overall air quality at a specific location and time.

The primary purpose of AQI is public health protection. By providing a simple, standardized way to communicate air quality information, AQI helps people:
- Make informed decisions about outdoor activities
- Understand health risks associated with air pollution
- Take protective measures when air quality is poor
- Track long-term air quality trends in their area

### History and Development

The concept of AQI was first developed in the United States in the 1960s by the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA). Since then, it has been adopted worldwide, with various countries adapting the index to their specific air quality standards and pollutant profiles.

In India, the Central Pollution Control Board (CPCB) manages the National Air Quality Index, which monitors and reports AQI across major cities. The system was upgraded in 2015 to provide real-time data through the AQI India portal and various mobile applications.

## How Does AQI Work?

### Pollutants Measured

The AQI doesn't measure air quality directly—it measures the concentration of specific pollutants that affect air quality. The standard AQI in India monitors six major pollutants:

| Pollutant | Full Name | Primary Sources |
|-----------|-----------|-----------------|
| PM10 | Particulate Matter (10 micrometers) | Dust, construction, vehicles |
| PM2.5 | Particulate Matter (2.5 micrometers) | Vehicles, industries, burning |
| O3 | Ozone | Chemical reactions, sunlight |
| NO2 | Nitrogen Dioxide | Vehicles, power plants |
| SO2 | Sulfur Dioxide | Industries, power plants |
| CO | Carbon Monoxide | Vehicles, incomplete burning |

### The Calculation Process

AQI calculation involves these steps:

1. **Measurement**: Air quality monitoring stations measure pollutant concentrations in real-time
2. **Conversion**: Each pollutant is converted to a sub-index value (0-500)
3. **Selection**: The highest sub-index becomes the overall AQI value
4. **Categorization**: The AQI is assigned to a color category and health message

This process ensures that the AQI reflects the most significant health risk at any given time. If PM2.5 levels are dangerously high but other pollutants are normal, the AQI will reflect the PM2.5 risk.

## AQI Categories and Health Implications

### The Six AQI Categories

The AQI is divided into six color-coded categories, each with specific health implications:

#### Good (0-50) - Green
- **Air Quality:** Satisfactory
- **Health Impact:** Little to no risk
- **Action:** Ideal for all outdoor activities

#### Satisfactory (51-100) - Yellow
- **Air Quality:** Acceptable
- **Health Impact:** Minor breathing discomfort for sensitive people
- **Action:** Sensitive individuals should reduce prolonged outdoor exertion

#### Moderately Polluted (101-200) - Orange
- **Air Quality:** Unhealthy for sensitive groups
- **Health Impact:** Breathing discomfort for people with lung diseases, children, elderly, and heart patients
- **Action:** Sensitive groups should limit outdoor exposure

#### Poor (201-300) - Red
- **Air Quality:** Unhealthy
- **Health Impact:** Breathing discomfort for most people; prolonged exposure may cause respiratory issues
- **Action:** Everyone should reduce prolonged outdoor exertion

#### Very Poor (301-400) - Purple
- **Air Quality:** Very Unhealthy
- **Health Impact:** Respiratory illness; prolonged exposure may cause health effects
- **Action:** Avoid outdoor activities; use air purifiers indoors

#### Severe (401-500+) - Maroon
- **Air Quality:** Hazardous
- **Health Impact:** Serious health effects for everyone; emergency conditions
- **Action:** Stay indoors; use N95 masks if going outside is unavoidable

### Understanding the Color Codes

The color-coded system provides instant visual recognition of air quality levels:

- **Green** = Safe to go outside
- **Yellow** = Moderate caution
- **Orange** = Sensitive groups take care
- **Red** = Everyone limit outdoor activity
- **Purple** = Stay indoors as much as possible
- **Maroon** = Emergency conditions

Most weather apps and AQI displays use these colors to help you quickly assess the situation without reading detailed numbers.

## Why AQI Matters for Indian Cities

### India's Air Quality Crisis

India faces severe air pollution challenges. According to multiple studies, several Indian cities consistently rank among the most polluted in the world. Factors contributing to this crisis include:

- **Vehicle Emissions:** Rapid urbanization and increasing vehicle ownership
- **Industrial Pollution:** Coal-based power plants and manufacturing
- **Agricultural Burning:** Seasonal stubble burning in northern India
- **Construction Dust:** Uncontrolled construction activities
- **Household Sources:** Biomass burning for cooking and heating

### Seasonal Patterns

Air quality in India follows distinct seasonal patterns:

**Winter (November-February):** Worst air quality due to temperature inversion, reduced wind speed, and increased biomass burning.

**Summer (March-June):** Moderate air quality with dust storms in some regions.

**Monsoon (July-September):** Best air quality due to rain washing away pollutants.

**Post-Monsoon (October):** Transition period with varying conditions.

Understanding these patterns helps you anticipate air quality changes and plan activities accordingly.

## How to Check AQI in Your City

### Online Resources

Several platforms provide real-time AQI data for Indian cities:

1. **AQI India (aqicn.org):** Comprehensive real-time AQI data for Indian cities
2. **CPCB Central Pollution Control Board:** Official government AQI monitoring
3. **SAFAR (System of Air Quality and Weather Forecasting):** Weather and AQI forecasts
4. **Mobile Apps:** Multiple apps provide AQI data with alerts

### What to Look For

When checking AQI data, pay attention to:
- **Current AQI value** and color category
- **Dominant pollutant** (which pollutant is driving the high AQI)
- **Trend** (improving or worsening)
- **Forecast** (expected changes in next few hours/days)

## Practical Tips for AQI-Based Decision Making

### Daily Routine Adjustments

**When AQI is Good (0-50):**
- Enjoy outdoor activities freely
- Open windows for ventilation
- Exercise outdoors as preferred

**When AQI is Satisfactory (51-100):**
- Normal activities are fine
- Sensitive individuals may want to limit intense outdoor exercise
- Keep windows open but monitor if you have respiratory issues

**When AQI is Moderately Polluted (101-200):**
- Reduce prolonged outdoor exertion
- Sensitive groups (children, elderly, heart/lung patients) should stay indoors
- Consider indoor exercise alternatives

**When AQI is Poor (201-300):**
- Limit all outdoor activities
- Use air purifiers indoors
- Wear N95 masks if going outside is necessary

**When AQI is Very Poor/Severe (301+):**
- Stay indoors as much as possible
- Use air purifiers with HEPA filters
- Avoid physical exertion
- Keep windows closed

### Protecting Vulnerable Groups

Certain populations are more susceptible to air pollution effects:
- **Children:** Developing lungs are more vulnerable
- **Elderly:** Reduced lung capacity and existing health conditions
- **Pregnant Women:** Air pollution can affect fetal development
- **People with Heart/Lung Conditions:** Asthma, COPD, heart disease
- **Outdoor Workers:** Prolonged exposure without protection

These groups should be especially cautious when AQI exceeds 100.

## FAQ: Understanding AQI

### What is the difference between AQI and PM2.5?
AQI is an overall index that combines multiple pollutants, while PM2.5 is just one specific pollutant (fine particulate matter). AQI takes the highest value among all measured pollutants, so sometimes AQI is high due to PM2.5, other times due to ozone or other pollutants.

### How often is AQI updated?
Real-time AQI is typically updated every hour or more frequently, depending on the monitoring station. Some platforms provide near real-time updates every few minutes.

### Why do different apps show different AQI values?
Different apps may use data from different monitoring stations, calculation methods, or time averaging periods. For the most accurate local AQI, check the official CPCB or state pollution control board websites.

### Is AQI the same worldwide?
No, different countries use slightly different AQI scales and pollutant combinations. India uses the CPCB AQI scale, which is based on the US EPA method but adapted for Indian conditions.

### Can I trust AQI forecasts?
AQI forecasts are generally reliable for 24-48 hours but can change due to unexpected weather events or emission sources. Always check the current AQI before planning outdoor activities.

### What should I do if AQI is "Severe"?
Stay indoors, keep windows closed, use air purifiers if available, and avoid physical exertion. If you must go outside, wear an N95 mask. Those with respiratory or heart conditions should consult their doctor.

### How can I reduce my personal air pollution exposure?
- Check AQI before outdoor activities
- Use indoor air purifiers
- Avoid outdoor exercise when AQI is high
- Keep windows closed during polluted periods
- Use vehicle air recirculation when driving in polluted areas
- Consider masks for outdoor activities during poor AQI days
