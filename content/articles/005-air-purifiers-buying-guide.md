## Why You Need an Air Purifier in India

### The Indian Air Pollution Reality

India is home to 14 of the world's 15 most polluted cities. According to the IQAir World Air Quality Report 2024, Delhi's annual average PM2.5 concentration was 92.7 μg/m³—nearly 18 times the WHO guideline of 5 μg/m³. Mumbai, Kolkata, and Chennai aren't far behind.

But outdoor pollution isn't the only threat. Indoor air can be 2-5 times more polluted than outdoor air due to:
- Cooking smoke (especially with gas stoves)
- Dust and allergens from outside
- Volatile organic compounds (VOCs) from furniture and paints
- Mold and bacteria in humid conditions
- Secondhand smoke and pet dander

### Health Impacts of Poor Indoor Air Quality

Exposure to PM2.5 and other pollutants is linked to:
- Respiratory diseases (asthma, COPD, bronchitis)
- Cardiovascular problems (heart attacks, strokes)
- Reduced lung function in children
- Increased risk of lung cancer
- Premature death (WHO estimates 7 million premature deaths annually from air pollution)

For vulnerable groups—children, elderly, pregnant women, and those with pre-existing conditions—the risks are even higher.

### When to Use an Air Purifier

You should consider using an air purifier when:
- **AQI is above 100 (Unhealthy for Sensitive Groups)**: Run purifiers in occupied rooms
- **AQI is above 150 (Unhealthy)**: Run purifiers continuously in main living areas
- **AQI is above 200 (Very Unhealthy)**: Run purifiers in all occupied rooms, keep windows closed
- **AQI is above 300 (Hazardous)**: Run purifiers 24/7, seal windows, limit time indoors without purification

Even when AQI is moderate (50-100), air purifiers can help reduce allergens, dust, and indoor pollutants that affect daily comfort.

## How Air Purifiers Work

Air purifiers use a combination of filters and technologies to remove contaminants from the air. Understanding these components helps you choose the right model.

### Core Filtration Technologies

**1. HEPA Filters (High-Efficiency Particulate Air)**
- Captures 99.97% of particles as small as 0.3 microns
- Removes PM2.5, PM10, dust, pollen, mold spores, and pet dander
- **True HEPA** vs **HEPA-type**: Only "True HEPA" meets the official standard; "HEPA-type" may be less effective

**2. Activated Carbon Filters**
- Absorbs gases, odors, and VOCs (cooking smells, smoke, chemicals)
- Essential for Indian homes with cooking fumes and pollution
- Thickness matters—more carbon = better gas absorption

**3. Pre-Filters**
- Captures large particles like hair and dust
- Extends the life of HEPA and carbon filters
- Washable and reusable

**4. Additional Technologies (Optional)**
- **UV-C Light**: Kills bacteria and viruses (useful but not essential)
- **Ionizers**: Release negative ions to attach to particles (controversial—may produce ozone)
- **Plasma/PCO**: Advanced oxidation for chemical pollutants (expensive, less proven)

### Understanding CADR (Clean Air Delivery Rate)

CADR measures how quickly an air purifier can clean a room. It's expressed in cubic meters per hour (m³/h) or cubic feet per minute (CFM).

**CADR Ratings for Different Pollutants:**
- **Dust**: Large particles (10+ microns)
- **Pollen**: Medium particles (5-10 microns)
- **Smoke**: Small particles (0.1-1 micron) — most relevant for PM2.5

**How to Calculate Required CADR:**
1. Measure your room's volume (Length × Width × Height in meters)
2. Multiply by 5 (recommended air changes per hour for polluted environments)
3. That's your minimum CADR requirement

**Example:** For a 150 sq ft (14 m²) bedroom with 8 ft (2.4 m) ceiling:
- Room volume = 14 × 2.4 = 33.6 m³
- Required CADR = 33.6 × 5 = 168 m³/h

### Room Size Considerations

Air purifiers are rated for specific room sizes. Always choose a model rated for at least 20% larger than your actual room size to account for:
- Open doorways and adjacent spaces
- Higher pollution levels in Indian cities
- Need for faster purification during peak AQI

## Key Features to Look For

### Filter Quality and Replacement Costs

**HEPA Filter Standards:**
- **True HEPA (H13)**: Captures 99.97% of 0.3-micron particles — **Recommended**
- **H14 HEPA**: Captures 99.995% — better but more expensive, usually for medical use
- **HEPA-type**: Avoid — may only capture 99% of larger particles

**Filter Lifespan in Indian Conditions:**
- HEPA filters: 6-12 months (depending on pollution levels)
- Carbon filters: 3-6 months (faster saturation due to cooking fumes)
- Pre-filters: Washable monthly

**Replacement Costs:**
- Budget models: ₹1,000-2,000 per filter set
- Mid-range: ₹2,000-4,000 per set
- Premium: ₹4,000-8,000 per set
- **Tip**: Calculate annual filter cost before buying — some budget models have expensive filters!

### Noise Levels

Air purifiers run continuously, so noise matters:
- **Sleep mode**: 25-35 dB (quieter than a whisper)
- **Normal operation**: 35-50 dB (similar to a conversation)
- **Maximum speed**: 50-65 dB (like a vacuum cleaner)

Look for models with a dedicated sleep mode that runs at <35 dB. Some premium models have automatic mode that adjusts speed based on air quality, keeping noise low when air is clean.

### Smart Features

**Essential Smart Features:**
- **Air quality sensors**: Real-time PM2.5 display
- **Auto mode**: Adjusts fan speed based on air quality
- **App control**: Monitor and control from smartphone

**Nice-to-Have Features:**
- **Voice control**: Works with Alexa/Google Assistant
- **Scheduling**: Set timers for operation
- **Filter replacement alerts**: Reminds you when to change filters
- **Multi-room monitoring**: Some systems can monitor multiple rooms

### Energy Efficiency

Air purifiers run 24/7, so energy consumption matters:
- **Wattage range**: 30W (sleep mode) to 70W (max speed)
- **Annual cost**: ₹1,500-3,000 depending on usage and electricity rates
- **Energy Star certified**: Look for models with low power consumption

## Maintenance and Filter Replacement

### Filter Lifespan in Indian Conditions

**HEPA Filters:**
- Normal conditions: 12 months
- High pollution cities (Delhi, Mumbai): 6-9 months
- **Signs you need replacement**: Reduced airflow, persistent odors, visible dirt

**Activated Carbon Filters:**
- Normal conditions: 6 months
- Indian cooking conditions: 3-4 months
- **Signs you need replacement**: Cooking smells linger, smoke isn't filtered

**Pre-Filters:**
- Wash every 2-4 weeks
- Vacuum weekly to extend life

### Cleaning and Maintenance Tips

1. **Weekly**: Vacuum the exterior and pre-filter
2. **Monthly**: Wash pre-filter with mild soap and water
3. **Every 3 months**: Check filter condition, replace if dirty
4. **Every 6 months**: Deep clean the unit, check for mold
5. **Annually**: Replace HEPA and carbon filters (or sooner in high pollution)

## Practical Tips for Using Air Purifiers

### Placement Guidelines

**Do:**
- Place in the center of the room or near the pollution source (e.g., near windows)
- Keep 3-5 feet away from walls and furniture
- Elevate on a table or stand for better air circulation
- Run continuously for best results

**Don't:**
- Place in corners or behind furniture
- Block air intake or exhaust
- Use in humid areas (bathrooms, kitchens) without proper ventilation

### When to Run Your Air Purifier

**Continuous Operation (Recommended):**
- Run 24/7 for consistent air quality
- Use auto mode to adjust speed based on pollution levels
- Sleep mode at night for quiet operation

**Peak Pollution Periods:**
- Morning (6-10 AM): Traffic pollution peaks
- Evening (6-10 PM): Cooking fumes and traffic
- Winter months: Higher pollution overall

### Combining with Other Measures

Air purifiers are most effective when combined with:
1. **Sealing windows and doors** during high AQI days
2. **Using N95 masks** when going outside
3. **Avoiding indoor pollution sources** (smoking, burning incense)
4. **Regular cleaning** to reduce dust and allergens
5. **Humidity control** (30-50% relative humidity)

## FAQ Section

**Q: Do air purifiers really work for PM2.5?**
A: Yes, air purifiers with True HEPA filters can remove 99.97% of PM2.5 particles from indoor air. Studies show they can reduce indoor PM2.5 by 50-90% depending on the model and room conditions.

**Q: How much does it cost to run an air purifier 24/7?**
A: A typical air purifier uses 30-50 watts on low speed. At ₹8 per kWh, running 24/7 costs about ₹60-100 per month. Premium models with energy-saving features cost even less.

**Q: Can I use an air purifier in a room with an AC?**
A: Yes, and it's highly recommended. ACs circulate air but don't filter PM2.5. Combining AC with an air purifier gives you cool, clean air. Place the purifier away from the AC vent for best results.

**Q: How often should I replace air purifier filters?**
A: In Indian cities with high pollution, replace HEPA filters every 6-9 months and carbon filters every 3-4 months. Some models have filter replacement indicators that alert you when it's time.

**Q: Are expensive air purifiers worth it?**
A: It depends on your needs. Budget models (₹8,000-15,000) work well for small to medium rooms. Premium models (₹30,000+) offer better filtration, quieter operation, and smart features. For large rooms or severe allergies, premium is worth it.

**Q: Can air purifiers remove viruses and bacteria?**
A: HEPA filters capture viruses and bacteria (they're larger than 0.3 microns). Some models add UV-C light for extra protection, but HEPA alone is sufficient for most homes.

**Q: Should I run the air purifier with windows open?**
A: No. Air purifiers work best in sealed environments. With windows open, polluted outdoor air constantly enters, making it hard for the purifier to keep up. Close windows during high AQI days.

**Q: What's the difference between air purifier and air conditioner?**
A: Air purifiers remove particles and pollutants from air. Air conditioners cool the air and may filter some large particles, but they're not designed to remove PM2.5 or gases. You need both for cool, clean air.
